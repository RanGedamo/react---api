Moshe

(November 21, 1984 - January 5, 1985) was an American-Sudanese-Israeli operation as part of a fraternal operation, to bring Ethiopian Jews to Israel, during which the Jews were transferred from the refugee camps in Sudan to Belgium and from there to Israel on civilian flights by TEA. As part of the operation, 6,364 Jews immigrated to Israel.

Shlomo

Operation Shlomo (Twelfth Sivan 5741, May 24-25, 1991) or in its original name Operation Heavy Rains was an air train operation to completely evacuate the Jews of Beta Israel who were concentrated in the city of Addis Ababa. Within 36 hours, 14,310 Jews were evacuated to Israel by 18 C-130 Hercules aircraft, Israeli Air Force Boeing 707s from the 120th Squadron (then called "International") as well as 23 El Al Boeing aircraft and one Ethiopian Airlines Ethiopian aircraft. The operation is named after King Solomon, who according to the Bible, met the Queen of Sheba.

There are no written sources about the origins of Ethiopian Jews and the historical time when they came to Ethiopia. For the most part, history has been preserved orally and has been passed down from generation to generation. But according to the hypothesis and the way of life that has been preserved, about three versions have been given regarding the question of their origin. The prevailing opinion about the origin of the Ethiopian Jews says that they are descendants of the tribe of Dan. In the days of the division between the kingdom of Israel and the kingdom of Judah, the tribe of Dan decided not to be drawn into a quarrel between the brothers and went down to Egypt and from there citrus with the Nile to the land of Kush in present day Ethiopia and Sudan.
